DMCA Copyright Policy

Last updated: July 6, 2026 · Version 2026-07-06

Pinfold (“we”, “us”, “our”) respects intellectual property rights and expects users of the Pinfold application (com.pinfoldai.fileexplorer), our APIs, and related services (collectively, the “Service”) to do the same. This policy explains how copyright owners may submit notices under the U.S. Digital Millennium Copyright Act (“DMCA”), 17 U.S.C. § 512, and how users may submit counter-notices. It supplements our Copyright & IP Policy and Content Ownership Policy.

1. Designated agent

Our designated agent for DMCA notices is:

Pinfold — DMCA Agent
Email: support@pinfoldai.com
Subject line: DMCA Notice

Only DMCA notices and counter-notices should be sent to this contact. For other legal inquiries, use the same address with an appropriate subject line.

2. Filing a DMCA takedown notice

If you believe that material accessible through the Service infringes your copyright, you may submit a written DMCA notice to our designated agent. To be effective, your notice must include:

  • Your physical or electronic signature (typing your full legal name at the bottom of an email constitutes an electronic signature)
  • Identification of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed, or a representative list if multiple works are covered by a single notification
  • Identification of the material you claim is infringing, with sufficient detail for us to locate it (for example, account email, URLs, file names, or publishing queue identifiers)
  • Your contact information: name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address
  • A statement that you have a good-faith belief that use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law
  • A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on the owner’s behalf

3. Our response to valid notices

When we receive a notice that substantially complies with the DMCA, we may:

  • Remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material
  • Notify the user who posted the material
  • Document the notice and our actions

We act expeditiously in appropriate circumstances. We are not able to adjudicate copyright disputes; we process notices that meet DMCA requirements.

4. Counter-notification

If you believe material was removed or disabled by mistake or misidentification, you may submit a DMCA counter-notice to our designated agent. Your counter-notice must include:

  • Your physical or electronic signature
  • Identification of the material that was removed and its location before removal
  • A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good-faith belief the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification
  • Your name, address, telephone number, and email address
  • A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for the judicial district in which your address is located (or, if outside the U.S., any district in which we may be found), and that you will accept service of process from the person who submitted the original DMCA notice or their agent

If we receive a valid counter-notice, we may forward it to the original complainant. Unless the complainant notifies us within ten (10) business days that they have filed a court action seeking to restrain the user from engaging in infringing activity, we may restore the material in accordance with the DMCA.

5. Repeat infringers

We maintain a policy of terminating, in appropriate circumstances, the accounts of users who are repeat infringers of copyright. A user may be deemed a repeat infringer after multiple valid DMCA notices or a pattern of infringing activity.

6. Misrepresentations

Under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f), any person who knowingly materially misrepresents that material is infringing, or that material was removed by mistake, may be liable for damages, including costs and attorneys’ fees. Submit notices and counter-notices in good faith only.

7. Non-U.S. rights holders

While the DMCA is U.S. law, we review copyright complaints from rights holders worldwide. Include the information listed in Section 2 so we can investigate regardless of your jurisdiction.

8. Trademarks and other IP

This policy addresses copyright under the DMCA. For trademark or other intellectual property complaints, email support@pinfoldai.com with details of the rights asserted and the allegedly infringing use. See Copyright & IP Policy.

9. No legal advice

This policy is provided for informational purposes and does not constitute legal advice. Consult an attorney if you are unsure whether material infringes copyright or whether a notice or counter-notice is appropriate.
