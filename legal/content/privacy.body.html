Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 6, 2026 · Version 2026-07-06

Pinfold AI (“Pinfold,” “we,” “us”) operates the Pinfold mobile application (com.pinfoldai.fileexplorer) for Android and iOS, our API at api.pinfoldai.com, and our website at pinfoldai.com. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal data, and describes your privacy rights. It applies to the App, Service, and marketing site unless a separate notice states otherwise.

1. Data controller and contact

Pinfold AI is the data controller for personal data processed through the Service, except where we act as a processor on behalf of a business customer under a separate agreement. For privacy inquiries, contact our Data Protection Officer at support@pinfoldai.com (subject line: Privacy inquiry) or use our contact form.

2. Scope and linked policies

This policy covers personal data we process when you use the App, create an Account, connect Third-Party Services, purchase a Subscription, visit our website, or contact support. It does not cover Third-Party Services you connect (Google, Apple, Facebook, cloud providers, social platforms, app stores, Stripe), which have their own privacy policies. See also our Cookie Policy, Terms of Service, and Data Deletion page.

4. How we collect data

  • Directly from you when you register, update your profile, connect integrations, publish content, purchase a Subscription, or contact support
  • Automatically when you use the App or website, including device information, logs, and analytics where enabled
  • From Third-Party Services when you sign in with Google, Apple, or Facebook, or connect cloud and social accounts
  • From payment processors (Google Play, Apple, Stripe) regarding transaction and subscription status

5. How we use personal data

  • Provide, operate, maintain, and improve the App and Service
  • Authenticate users and manage Accounts across devices
  • Process Subscriptions, verify entitlements, and prevent billing fraud
  • Enable cloud sync, social publishing, and AI features you request
  • Monitor security, detect abuse, enforce our Acceptable Use Policy and Terms
  • Respond to support requests, complaints, and legal obligations
  • Send service-related notices (security alerts, policy updates, billing confirmations)
  • Analyze aggregated or de-identified usage to improve product quality

We do not sell your personal data. We do not use your personal data for third-party advertising profiling.

6. Legal bases for processing (GDPR / EEA)

If you are in the European Economic Area, we process personal data on the following legal bases under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)): to provide the Service, manage your Account, process Subscriptions, and deliver features you request
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): security, fraud prevention, abuse detection, product improvement, and limited analytics, balanced against your rights
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): optional features, marketing communications where required, and non-essential cookies on our website
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): tax, accounting, regulatory requests, and compliance with applicable law

7. United Kingdom (UK GDPR)

If you are in the United Kingdom, the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018 apply. The legal bases, rights, and safeguards described in this policy apply to you. You may lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk. Our international transfer safeguards described below apply to UK data transfers.

8. Sharing and disclosure

We share personal data only as described below:

  • Social platforms (Facebook, Instagram, Threads, X, TikTok, LinkedIn, etc.) when you connect accounts and publish Content through official APIs
  • Cloud providers (Google, Microsoft, Dropbox) when you enable sync integrations
  • Authentication providers (Google, Apple, Facebook) when you use social login
  • Payment processors (Google Play, Apple App Store, Stripe) for Subscription billing
  • Infrastructure providers (including Azure and Hetzner) that host our .NET backend and store encrypted data
  • Analytics and crash reporting providers where enabled, subject to their policies and our minimization practices
  • Professional advisers (legal, accounting) under confidentiality obligations
  • Law enforcement and regulators when required by valid legal process or to protect rights, safety, and security
  • Business transfers in connection with merger, acquisition, or asset sale, with notice where required by law

9. International data transfers

Personal data may be processed in countries where we or our service providers operate, including members of the EEA, the United Kingdom, the United States, and other jurisdictions. Where required, we implement appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), the UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or Addendum, and supplementary measures where necessary. You may request information about transfer safeguards by contacting our DPO.

10. Data retention

  • Account data is retained while your Account is active and for a reasonable period afterward for backup, dispute resolution, and legal compliance
  • Temporary social upload copies are deleted after publishing completes or within 30 days, whichever is sooner
  • OAuth tokens are deleted when you disconnect an integration or delete your Account
  • Billing and transaction records may be retained as required for tax, accounting, and fraud prevention (typically up to 7 years where required)
  • Support tickets and abuse reports may be retained to handle follow-up and enforce policies
  • Logs and analytics data are retained for limited periods based on operational need, then deleted or aggregated

11. Security measures

We implement technical and organizational measures including HTTPS encryption in transit, encrypted token storage on servers, access controls, and industry-standard security practices. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. You are responsible for your device passcode, app lock PIN, vault passphrase, and security of connected Third-Party Service accounts. Report suspected security issues to support@pinfoldai.com promptly.

14. California (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) provides additional rights:

  • Right to know what personal information we collect, use, disclose, and retain
  • Right to delete personal information, subject to exceptions
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing of personal information — we do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information — we use sensitive information only as necessary to provide the Service
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights

Submit California requests to support@pinfoldai.com with subject “California Privacy Request.” Authorized agents may submit requests with proof of authorization. We will verify identity before responding.

15. Brazil (LGPD)

If you are in Brazil, the Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (LGPD) grants rights including confirmation of processing, access, correction, anonymization or deletion, portability, information about sharing, revocation of consent, and review of automated decisions. Our legal bases under LGPD include consent, contract performance, legitimate interests, credit protection, and compliance with legal obligations. Contact our DPO to exercise LGPD rights or raise concerns with the Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados (ANPD).

16. Canada (PIPEDA)

If you are in Canada, personal information is handled in accordance with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and applicable provincial laws. You may access, correct, and challenge our compliance by contacting support@pinfoldai.com. You may also contact the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada if concerns are unresolved.

17. Australia (Privacy Act 1988)

If you are in Australia, we handle personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). You may request access to or correction of personal information we hold. Complaints may be submitted to us first; if unresolved, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).

18. AI and automated processing

AI features may process text, file metadata, or excerpts you submit to generate suggestions, captions, or organizational recommendations. Processing may occur on-device or on our servers hosted on Azure or Hetzner infrastructure. We may log prompts and outputs in limited form for safety, quality assurance, and abuse prevention. We do not use your private file library to train public AI models without explicit consent. Review AI outputs before use; automated suggestions are not guaranteed to be accurate.

19. Cookies and similar technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies as described in our Cookie Policy. The App may use local storage and device identifiers for session management and analytics. You can manage website cookie preferences through our consent banner where available.

20. Children’s privacy

Pinfold is not directed at children under 13 (or under 16 in the EEA/UK where applicable). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided personal data, contact support@pinfoldai.com and we will take steps to delete it.
